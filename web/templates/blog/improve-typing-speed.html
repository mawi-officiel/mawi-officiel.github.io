How to Professionally Improve Your Typing Speed

In our fast-paced digital world, typing speed is not just a bonus skill, but a necessity for increasing productivity and reducing strain. This guide puts you on the right path to mastering touch typing.

What is Touch Typing and Why Does it Matter?

Touch Typing is typing without looking at the keyboard, relying on muscle memory. This method allows you to focus on the screen and content rather than hunting for keys.

  • speedHigher Speed: Professionals can exceed 100 WPM.
  • check_circleGreater Accuracy: Reduced typos thanks to muscle memory.
  • health_and_safetyBetter Health: Reduced neck and eye strain.

Correct Posture: The Foundation of Speed

Before you touch the keys, check your posture. Poor posture causes fatigue and reduces speed.

chair

Straight Back

Keep your back straight and feet flat on the floor.

visibility

Eye Level

Screen should be at eye level or slightly lower.

front_hand

Hand Position

Elbows at 90 degrees, wrists floating above the desk.

Golden Tips for Fast Improvement

  • starAccuracy first, then speed. Speed comes naturally with time, but correcting errors takes longer.
  • visibility_offNever look at the keyboard. Cover your hands with a cloth if necessary to force your brain to memorize.
  • scheduleShort daily practice (15 mins) is better than long, sporadic sessions.
